2026 Conference Speakers

Scholars & practitioners.

The national scholars and practitioners who will share their expertise on bullying prevention, school climate, and student wellbeing at this year's conference.

Raquel
Sosa, PhD
Speaker

Raquel Sosa, PhD

Dr. Raquel Sosa (she/her) is an Assistant Professor in Psychology at The College of New Jersey (TCNJ). She earned her PhD in Counseling Psychology from Lehigh University. Raquel is a daughter of Mexican immigrants, and her career interests are heavily tied to her personal values and life experiences.

Her research lab, the Latine Liberation lab, focuses on how culturally responsive methods like liberation psychology and critical consciousness facilitate positive outcomes among Latinx people. Clinically, she has experience providing therapy in a variety of settings including college counseling, academic medical centers, and K–12 schools with children and adults.

Tom
McHale
Speaker

Tom McHale

Tom McHale has taught English and journalism at a large public high school in New Jersey for over 20 years. He is a graduate of Temple University and holds a Master's degree in English Ed from Arcadia University and a Masters in Journalism Education from Kent State University.

Tom is the president of the Garden State Scholastic Press Association and has worked to pass student journalists' rights legislation through that organization. Tom focuses on how to empower every student to use their voice to advocate for themselves and others, and his work on student rights, student voice, and antiracist education are an integral part of that. He hosts the Helping Empower Antiracism Teachers podcast for NJHEART.

Liz
McHugh, PhD
Speaker

Liz McHugh, Ph.D.

Dr. Liz McHugh is a clinical and school psychologist with the New Jersey Pediatric Psychiatry Collaborative (PPC). She is also an Assistant Professor at Hackensack School of Medicine and trains future psychologists in assessment, collaboration, and supporting children in their schools.

Dr. McHugh provides thorough neurodevelopmental assessments to children and adolescents as part of the collaborative care model at the PPC. She is also part of the 0–5 Center of Excellence at the PPC which aims to support the youngest children and their families. The aim of the PPC is to empower pediatric care providers to manage patients with mental health needs through collaboration.

As a school psychologist by training, Dr. McHugh is passionate about supporting children's social-emotional needs in school so that they can thrive as learners. Her approach emphasizes connecting families with evidence-based treatments through their primary care providers and school-based resources.

Julia Ann
Starega, LCSW
Speaker

Julia Ann Starega, MSW, LCSW

Julie Starega is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker and Founder of Healing Hearts, Growing Minds Counseling, LLC. With over 15 years of experience supporting children, adolescents, and families across school, hospital, and private practice settings, Julia specializes in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), parent coaching, play-based and creative child therapy, and strength-based, repair-oriented approaches that make therapy both effective and engaging.

Julia is deeply passionate about working with children and families because she believes in the power of healing, resilience, and the incredible potential for growth within every child. She believes therapy should feel supportive, empowering, and growth-centered for both children and their caregivers.
