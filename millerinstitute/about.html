Our Leadership & Mission

Leadership grounded
in practice.

CMI is led by practitioners with decades of combined experience in education, psychology, policy, and leadership development — and a deep commitment to the communities we serve.

Dr. Christopher C. Irving
Executive Director

Dr. Christopher C. Irving

Dr. Christopher C. Irving, a proud product of Paterson, NJ, is the Executive Director of the Ceceilyn Miller Institute. He holds a Bachelor's in Political Science from Ramapo College of New Jersey, a Master's in Public Policy and Administration from William Paterson University, and an EdD from Seton Hall University (2016).

Dr. Irving served for seven years on the Paterson Board of Education and is a co-founder of the National Student Leadership Diversity Convention (NSLDC). His work sits at the intersection of educational policy, community organizing, and the professional development of leaders who look like the communities they serve.

Dr. Judith Springer, Psy.D.
Associate Director

Dr. Judith Springer, Psy.D.

Dr. Judith Springer is a licensed psychologist and school psychologist who serves as CMI's Associate Director. Her career bridges clinical practice, school consultation, and professional training.

Dr. Springer is a co-author — with Maureen Underwood — of a widely used youth-suicide-prevention book published by Hazelden (editions in 2011, 2013, and 2018). Her combined expertise in clinical psychology and school systems informs CMI's programming across K–12 and higher-education settings.

How We Engage

Every program
includes.

CMI's program fee is designed to be transparent and inclusive. Every engagement includes:

  • Program Planning Consultation — scoping the work with your team
  • One Full Day Training Session — delivered on site
  • One to two Senior CMI Trainers — for depth and responsiveness
  • Program Administration — logistics, materials, and follow-up
