Mission & Learning Outcomes

Equipping student leaders
to shape campus.

NSLDC provides student leaders with the awareness, content knowledge, and skills to foster staff development and mutual respect when exploring issues of diversity.

Our Approach

From awareness, to knowledge,
to action.

Through experiential workshops, keynote sessions, and roundtable discussions, teams of students explore various elements of diversity and how they affect themselves and their campuses. During the conference, students develop a Campus Action Plan to bring a piece of the NSLDC experience back to their own campuses.

Learning Outcomes · Area 1

Raise Awareness.

Participants will leave with heightened awareness of diversity, identity, and the dynamics that shape campus life.

  • Recognize personal biases and assumptions
  • Understand intersectionality and how identities overlap
  • Develop empathy across lines of difference
  • See themselves as agents of social change
Learning Outcomes · Area 2

Increase Knowledge.

Participants will expand their content knowledge of historical, social, and cultural contexts.

  • History and lived experiences of Black Americans
  • Global diversity and cross-cultural understanding
  • A shared vocabulary for naming and discussing identity
  • The structural roots of inequity on college campuses
Learning Outcomes · Area 3

Develop Skills.

Participants will build the practical skills of inclusive leadership.

  • Inclusive leadership and facilitation
  • Asking open-ended questions that invite dialogue
  • Standing up — respectfully and effectively
  • Building coalitions across identity lines
Learning Outcomes · Area 4

Plan for Social Action.

Every team leaves with a concrete Campus Action Plan — the heart of the NSLDC experience.

  • Service projects tied to real campus needs
  • Cross-cultural interaction and sustained dialogue
  • Programming that shifts campus climate
  • Accountability structures to see the plan through
